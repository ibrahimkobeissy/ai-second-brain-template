# Method — Problem-Framing Canvas (PRD 1.9)

*Re-authored from Dean Peters' Product-Manager-Skills (`problem-framing-canvas`), CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 — attribution per Build Stance.*

MITRE's Look Inward / Look Outward / Reframe sequence — used here to surface what you *don't* know before committing. The goal of 1.9 is honesty, not confidence.

## Apply to PRD 1.9 (Assumptions & Open Questions)
- **Assumptions** — what must be true for this to work (market exists, willingness-to-pay, feasibility).
- **Unknowns** — what you can't yet answer; explicitly mark the **least-confident** ones.
- **Specific questions** — phrased so a Phase-2 analysis or a customer interview could answer them.
- These directly seed Risk Assessment + Idea Validation in Phase 2.

**Anti-pattern:** an empty or rosy 1.9. If everything is "assumed fine", the framing is dishonest — the riskiest assumption is the one you didn't write down.
