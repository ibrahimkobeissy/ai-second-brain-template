# Method — Proto-Persona (PRD 1.2)

*Re-authored from Dean Peters' Product-Manager-Skills (`proto-persona`, `jobs-to-be-done`), CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 — attribution per Build Stance.*

A proto-persona is a *hypothesis* of a target customer, built from what you already know (not yet interviews). Capture: segment, a one-line portrait, their **job-to-be-done** (the progress they're trying to make), top pains, and current alternatives.

## Apply to PRD 1.2 (Target Market & Customer Profile)
- Name 1–2 **primary** segments — don't spread thin.
- Per segment: demographics/firmographics, geography, B2B/B2C, the job-to-be-done, and the top 1–3 pains (tie back to 1.1).
- Mark each as a **hypothesis** to validate in Phase 2 (Customer Discovery / Idea Validation).

**Anti-pattern:** "everyone" as the market. A persona you can't picture interviewing is too vague.
