# DIY automation — demand signal + the trust gap

> [!info] Source
> [How I Automated My Standup Meetings](https://dev.to/get_pieces/how-i-automated-my-standup-meetings-and-why-you-should-too-4aa8) — DEV Community

## TL;DR
Devs are already hand-rolling auto-standups — proof the pain is real, but also proof the *draft* alone is a weekend project, so a paid product must sell something harder: trust.

## Why it's relevant to [[standup-tools]]
Both a green light (validated demand) and a red flag (low willingness-to-pay for the commodity part). Sharpens where a defensible product would have to sit.

## What we can steal
- **Demand validation** — people build this themselves → the job-to-be-done is real (`value-proposition`, `idea-validation`).
- **The commoditization warning** — "draft from commits" is an afternoon's work → don't price or position on the draft itself (`financial-model`, `competitive-differentiation`).
- **The trust gap as the wedge** — drafts list *what* changed, not *why it matters / what's blocked*; a tool that produces send-unedited updates is the unsolved problem.
- **Context beyond commits** (calendar + tickets) materially improves the draft → a feature-depth axis.

## What probably doesn't apply
- The specific DIY stack (one dev's script) — anecdote, not architecture.
